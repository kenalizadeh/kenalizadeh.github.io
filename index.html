Boids Implementation

Implemented a simulation of Boids, a flocking behavior model described by Craig Reynolds in his 1987 paper "Flocks, Herds, and Schools: A Distributed Behavioral Model". The Boids algorithm simulates the behavior of birds or fish in a group by applying three basic rules: separation (avoid crowding), alignment (steer towards average heading of neighbors), and cohesion (move towards average position of neighbors). The project showcases the dynamic interactions and emergent patterns that arise from these simple rules, demonstrating complex group behavior in a visually engaging way.
